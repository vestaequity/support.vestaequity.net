      Additional Yield Tools

      Vesta Equity offers a range of unique product features that allow homeowners to amplify the ROI on their listing and create a competitive offering for investors.

      Vesta Equityâ€™s yield tools enable homeowners to fine tune their listings and offer more bonus equity as part of every transaction. This allows a homeowner to customize their offering to compete with the wide array of investment options that investors have access to. Designating additional yield during the listing process also attracts investors who can see the deal parameters while browsing. Without designating additional yield, investors would instead need to make an offer to stay competitive with investment goals. 

      • For homeowners, these tools have the potential to make a listing more attractive to investors and reduce the amount of time it takes to receive funds.
      • For property investors, the yield tools will increase returns while reducing investment risk.

      Equity Dividend

      Equity Dividend is a yield-generating feature that allows investors to make an initial purchase for a fixed amount of equity, and then earn additional equity over the lifetime of the investment. When a listing is created, the homeowner determines how much additional equity to offer on an annual basis. After the equity purchase - a cash exchange for a fixed amount of equity - the Equity Dividend is then distributed as a monthly yield in the form of more equity over a 1-year to 20-year term. For example, an offering for a 30% equity stake in a $500,000 property with a 1% Equity Dividend would create a 50% position in the asset after 20 years (the initial 30% purchase plus 1% per year for 20 years). The Equity Dividend is awarded pro rata on the amount purchased relative to the amount offered. Using the same example, an investor purchasing all 30% would receive all of the Equity Dividend (1% per year) while two investors each purchasing 15% would each receive half of the Equity Dividend (0.5% per year).

      A familiar comparison to Vesta Equity's Equity Dividend is a cash dividend from a rental property where investors with fractional interests would receive a portion of the property's rent in the form of cash. In Vesta Equity's case, homeowners using their principal residences can offer more of what they have - home equity.

      Inducement Equity

      An Inducement Equity is an additional equity percentage granted to the investor at the point of purchase of the transaction. When a listing is created, the homeowner determines the additional amount of equity they want to offer and it becomes part of their offering on the published listing. For example, an offering for a 30% equity stake in a $500,000 property with a 1% Inducement Equity, would effectively offer 31% of equity for cash value of 30%. During the listing setup, the homeowner can toggle whether Inducement Equity is negotiable at the offer stage, and if so, the homeowner and investor can negotiate on how much of the available Inducement Equity to award per offer. Using the same example as above, an investor making the first offer for 10% equity stake of the available 30% might negotiate 50% of the available Inducement Equity (an additional 0.5%) rather than the pro rata 0.333%.
