      Creating a Listing for Your Property

      A walkthrough detailing the listing process and everything needed to get started on listing your equity.

      Once the account setup steps are complete, you can begin the process of creating a listing as a homeowner. This will involve:
      • Address submission.
      • Uploading property insurance.
      • Booking photography and evaluation.
      • Setting up your listing.
      • Executing closing agreements.
      • Publishing your listing to the marketplace.

      Address Submission

      The first step in creating a listing is to submit your address. Vesta Equity will validate that the address of the property is real and that we have access to property details such as market value and property specifications.

       

      Is your home eligible for Vesta Equity?

      If your property meets the following criteria then you can proceed with creating a listing:

      • Residential property in the US.
      • Owned free and clear, or still maintains a mortgage.
      • The property has valid insurance.
      • The Vesta Equity account belongs to an owner of the property on title.

      Uploading Property Insurance

      Following address submission you'll be prompted to upload a proof of insurance document.

      Is your property insurance valid?

      The following is a general list of required criteria for property insurance. For full details contact Customer Service to review our Equity Sharing Agreement:

      • Policy of "all risk" casualty insurance acceptable to Vesta Equity.
      • If, based on the property location, there is a 25% or greater chance of a flood over a 30-year period, a flood insurance policy equal to the full replacement value of the insured elements.
      • A policy providing coverage for Property-related liabilities of all owners of the Property with policy limits not less than $500,000 per incident.

      Booking Photography and Evaluation

      Once the property insurance is uploaded, you'll be presented with an initial market value estimate for the property. This value is an AVM (automated valuation model) that takes into account the latest data from public record and MLS.

      Onboarding for every property requires an evaluation which you can book directly from our platform. The evaluation will be conducted through a live evaluation on the interior and exterior of the property, and will produce a comprehensive, condition-informed report, available for download on the listing.

      Have you made upgrades to the property since purchasing?

      The live evaluation will account for your upgrades and will factor that into the property's valuation!

      We also offer professional photography services, delivered through a network of providers nationwide, because we want property investors on the platform to view the property in its best light. While this is an optional service, it's highly recommended because great media will substantiate and enhance the property's investment potential.

      Prep your home to get the most out of photography!

      Most professional photography services will present you with a prep list that ensures the property will look its best. These include items like decluttering, removing trash cans, removing personal items, and so on. You can also check out our guide on do's and don'ts.

      How much does the evaluation and photography cost?

      Exact costs will be on directly available at on the Photography and Evaluation tab but below are estimates:

      • Evaluation: $175-$200
      • Photography: $350-$450

      Note that these costs are directly paid to Vesta Equity's service providers and we don't earn any revenue from them. Cancelation fees apply where we're charged for those.

      Following payment, these service providers will contact you directly and schedule sessions at your convenience and you'll be asked to facilitate entry for each. 

      Can the evaluation and photography be done at the same time?

      These are two separate services and each require separate access to the property on the day and times scheduled. You can certainly coordinate them one after the other to minimize any disruption but please ensure that each has enough time to complete their work and deliver the best service to you. 

      During this stage, our Closing team will also order a preliminary title report to verify the property's title records and prepare your title policy order which will be payable at the Closing step. A member of our Customer Success team will reach out to you to gather the following information:

      • If there are any other co-owners on the property such as a spouse, and if so, their full legal name.
      • The amount of equity you're looking to sell. By default, and unless otherwise notified, the maximum amount of equity a homeowner can sell is 80%. If you're looking to sell less, we can cap this and reduce the cost of the title policy. If you'd like to increase the amount afterwards then administrative fees would apply.

      Executing Closing Agreements

      After reviewing the preliminary title report, our Closing team will return a quote for a title policy along with legal fees. On proceeding with payment you'll also be prompted to contact Customer Service for copies of the Equity Sharing Agreement, Deed of Trust and Title Policy documents to review. 



      A summary of these documents is below. Please reference them for exact terms and conditions, consulting with your legal professional as required.

      • The Equity Sharing Agreement, or ESA, is the primary contract representing the deal between you as the homeowner and property investors, who are organized together as an LLC entity (Investor LLC) specific for your property. The ESA describes the nature of the transaction whereby the homeowner sells a percentage of equity, realized when the homeowner sells the property outright, in exchange for funds received from property investors. The ESA also outlines the rights and obligations of each party, and for the homeowner includes requirements such as upkeep of the property, maintaining property insurance, and payment of property taxes. Vesta Equity, LLC is also a party to the agreement in the role of the Manager of the Investor LLC entity. Once executed and secured by the Deed of Trust, the ESA can primarily be exited through selling the property. Homeowners can also exit by exercising an option for early repayment, or by repurchasing the equity on a secondary basis from property investors who hold those assets.
      • The Deed of Trust is a standard legal instrument in real estate transactions. On the Vesta Property Marketplace, the Deed of Trust grants investors a legally binding interest in a property. It does this by securing the ESA, creating a lien on the property, and conveying legal title to the Trustee party who will be an independent title company servicing the transaction and providing the title policy. When the terms of the ESA are fulfilled on a termination event, then Vesta Equity instructs the Trustee to release the lien on the property. The Deed of Trust requires notarization and, subsequently, it becomes recorded with the county registry local to the property.
      • The Title Policy is a form of insurance that protects property investors against any financial loss due to defects in title. Examples of these events are unknown back taxes that are owing, liens or other claims that arise, or even flaws in record ownership. The Title Policy confirms to property investors that title to the property is clear and that the homeowner is a party that is authorized to transact with them.

      A typical example of what happens when you sell your property.

      Let's say your property was valued at $500,000 at the time you listed its equity with Vesta Equity, and you sold 20% of it at market value ($100,000), to property investors on Vesta Equity's platform. A few years later, you want to sell the property and have an offer from a buyer at $600,000 which is market value at that time. You would:

      1. Provide notice to Vesta Equity at a minimum of 60 days prior to closing.
      2. Vesta Equity would return a notice at a minimum of 45 days prior to closing indicating the valuation of the property ($600,000) and the amount repayable to investors ($120,000, or 20% of $600,000).
      3. You make the repayment of $120,000 to investors via Vesta Equity. This can be coordinated directly from escrow on the sale.
      4. With the terms of the ESA now fulfilled, Vesta Equity instructs the Trustee to release the lien and re-convey legal title to you, allowing you to complete the sale and ending the contractual agreement between you and Vesta Equity.

      Some other documents may be included with this document package as well. These are:

      • A privacy disclosure which describes what kind of data our title services collect and send.
      • An Owner's Affidavit which is a declaration on the property from the homeowner.
      • An authorization letter that formally gives Vesta Equity the permission to record the Deed of Trust.

      You'll be able to access and execute these documents, customized for your deal, in the Closing step of setting up your listing. The Deed of Trust and Owner's Affidavit require notarization and Vesta Equity's Customer Success team will coordinate this where your local county office accepts remote online notarization (RON). In this case, a secure online session will be set up where you can execute the Deed of Trust in the presence of a notary. Where your local county requires traditional notarization offline, you can download the Deed of Trust and arrange notary services. UPS, for example, has thousands of locations across the US and will also mail the notarized document to Vesta Equity for closing, while many local banks also offer notary services. During the Closing step, Vesta Equity Customer Success will reach out to you and confirm the method of notarization as well as the mailing address to send the notarized Deed of Trust to where a traditional notary is required.

      To complete the Closing step, Vesta Equity will have to be added as an additional insured (eg. mortgagee/loss payee) on your property insurance. On insurable events that result in a devaluation of the property, and where Vesta Equity receives proceeds as compensation, this allows us to disburse those funds back to you in order to restore the property to its original condition and valuation. In cases where that isn't possible, we can then disburse funds back to investors instead to the extent of their interests in the property.

      How much does closing cost?

      Closing costs cover every expense needed to legally contract and secure the property. This includes legal fees, the cost of the title policy itself, notarization, and recording with the county. The cost range depends on your property's location and the value of the title policy. Most homeowners are approved for selling a maximum of 80% of their equity and the title policy is based on this percentage of the property's valuation at the time of the policy. If you'd like to sell a smaller amount, we can reduce the maximum percentage and lower the cost of the title policy. If you need to increase the maximum later, we can increase the policy and some additional administrative fees may apply at that time. The Closing step will list the exact costs required and some estimates are below:

      • Legal: $450-$550
      • Title Policy (based on average amount and location): $1,000-$2,000
      • Notary and Recording: $50-$100

      Setting Up Your Listing

      With the Closing step complete, you can begin setting up your listing. Vesta Equity's platform gathers all of the property specs behind the scenes so all that you need to do is organize your media and enter an optional description to complement the financials on the listing.

      You can upload photos and videos for your property by simply clicking the Add More button. The uppermost image will be the listing's thumbnail on the marketplace, while the two directly below it will be shown as secondary thumbnails on the listing page itself. Additional images will be available to the viewer as an image carousel when clicking on any image on the listing. If you purchased photography earlier, those images will be available for you to organize when you get to this stage.




      Description tips.

      Keep in mind that property investors are looking at the listing as a potential financial asset rather than as a residence. This means that features like amenities and walkability are probably less important unless they're used to support the current and future valuation of the property. On the other hand, characteristics such as maintenance of the property and potential upgrades help investors to know that their asset is being taken care of. As a homeowner, you'll also have insights into what's driving the value of your property and its surrounding area that no one else has. You can use the description field to convey that unique perspective to property investors. For any tips or suggestions just reach out to customersuccess@vestaequity.net. Also take a look at this article for additional guidance : "Drafting a high quality property description."

      Publishing Your Listing to the Marketplace

      Your last step on your listing is add the percentage of equity you want to sell. You can adjust this on a slider up to the maximum amount you were approved for. Equity on listings can be sold in 0.01% increments, so you can either:

      • Use the slider to pick the exact percentage you want.
      • Enter the dollar amount you're looking for which will adjust to the nearest 0.01% increment.

      A final preview shows you all of the components of the listing where you can make any edits before publishing it. Once you're ready, simply hit the Publish button and your listing will be live on the marketplace!
